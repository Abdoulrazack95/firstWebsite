Djibouti the crossroad of three continents

A travel guide to Djibouti

Covering an area of ​​approximately 23,200 km2, Djibouti is located in the part of Africa called the Horn of Africa, precisely on the west side of the southern outlet of the Red Sea. Former French colony, Djibouti is now a free and independent country. Small landlocked state between Ethiopia, Eritrea and Somalia, Djibouti rejoiced to its opening on the Red Sea. At the crossroads of three continents, Djibouti has long been a commercial and cultural crossroads. However Djibouti is above all breathtaking landscapes. Volcanoes, salt floes, rock deserts, exceptional seabed, sandy beaches ... The meeting place for tectonic plates from the Middle East and Africa boiling with life and color.

Landform

The landscapes, among the most remarkable in the world, offered to tourists in the interior of the country, range from canyons battered by the winds and particularly rugged mountains in the north to hot springs and volcanic decorations in the south and the west of the country, real lunar landscapes.

Nature

Beautiful beaches and amusement parks welcome you for your enjoyment. Discover Khor Ambado Beach, a beautifully landscaped beach. Day Forest National Park in the city of Djibouti will certainly give you pleasure.

Sport

Water sports invite you to meet in Djibouti. Kitesurf in a fairly simple, but professional way through Djibouti Kitesurf, a club where kitesurfing is the primary vocation. For even more fun adventures, visit the Rushing Waters Adventures; you will only be satisfied.
